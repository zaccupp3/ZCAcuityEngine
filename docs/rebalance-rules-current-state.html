Rebalancing + Assignment Rules

Audience: Leaders explaining platform behavior to staff unfamiliar with charge nursing assignment software.

Current state summary generated from active app logic in app/app.assignmentsrender.js, app/app.assignmentRules.js, and app/app.assignmentsDrag.js.

1) What the system is trying to optimize

Primary goal: safe and fair assignments, not just equal patient counts.

  • Reduce avoidable hard-rule violations first.
  • Then reduce report-source fragmentation (continuity/handoff burden).
  • Then tighten count spread where safe (example: 5/3 to 4/4 when legal).
  • Never override explicit lock/pin constraints.

2) Key concepts in plain language

Hard-rule violations

Assignments that break role rules/restrictions (RN or PCA restrictions, sitter constraints, etc.).

Highest priority to avoid

Report-source spread

How many previous owners a staff member inherits patients from. Fewer sources means cleaner handoff.

Continuity priority

Count spread

Difference between highest and lowest patient counts in same role group.

Fairness priority (when safe)

Pinned RN patient

A patient locked to a specific oncoming RN. Rebalance cannot move pinned patients unless manually unpinned first.

Hard lock

3) Populate flow (Oncoming)

StepWhat happensWhy
1. Reset and validate Clears RN/PCA assignment arrays and confirms active patients/staff exist. Avoid stale assignment carryover.
2. Apply RN pins first Pinned patients are placed directly onto locked RN; those patients are removed from RN distribution pool. RN continuity is explicit and honored before balancing logic.
3. Enforce sitter designations Sitter-designated PCA with room pair (e.g., 213) receives only sitter-tagged patients from that pair (max 2). Those patients are removed from non-sitter PCA pool. Protect sitter role boundaries and room-pair intent.
4. Distribute remaining patients Uses rule-aware distribute engine for RN/PCA. Balanced + rules-aware initial assignment.
5. Post-balance/repair passes Runs count-leveling and repair passes, then re-enforces sitter exclusivity. Clean up unevenness while preserving rules.

4) Rebalance (Both) flow - current priority order

RN and PCA rebalance are run independently (PCA rebalance excludes sitter-designated PCAs, then sitter exclusivity is re-applied).

PriorityEngine behaviorNotes
1 Safe rebalance gate: proposed changes must not produce avoidable violations and must improve optimization tuple. Implemented through rebalanceOwnersSafely().
2 Count-priority fallback: if safe engine does not change enough, count-leveling pass attempts legal moves to reduce spread. Recent update to reduce false “no safe move” outcomes on full units.
3 Re-apply sitter exclusivity: sitter-pair patients remain assigned only to their designated sitter PCA. Prevents drift after balancing passes.
Always RN pin lock: pinned RN patients are non-movable in rebalance. User must unpin manually before rebalance can relocate that patient.

5) “No safe rebalance improvement found” means

  • No legal move exists that improves objective(s) without violating hard gates.
  • Candidate moves may be blocked by RN pin locks, sitter constraints, or role restrictions.
  • With current updates, this should be less likely when a simple safe census move is available.

8) Practical interpretation for non-technical staff

If a team asks, “Why didn’t rebalance move this patient?” the common reasons are:

  • Patient is RN-pinned and cannot move until unlocked.
  • Patient is sitter-designated and tied to specific sitter room-pair owner.
  • Move would create a hard-rule break (restriction mismatch or unsafe pattern).
  • Move worsens continuity/report-source burden with no compensating safety gain.

Operational message: Rebalance is “safe optimization,” not “force equal counts at any cost.”

9) Known current tuning direction

  • Continue reducing false negatives where obvious count-equalizing moves are legal.
  • Keep RN pin and sitter constraints strict to preserve clinical intent.
  • Show clearer user feedback about what changed and why.
